Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you're wondering about The Wedding Business School

Straight answers on eligibility, curriculum, placements and life on campus — reach out if yours isn't here.

1. Why did IIWM change its name so soon after launching?

As we introduced IIWM to students and the industry, we realised our vision extended well beyond wedding management. Our institution is designed to prepare professionals for every aspect of the wedding industry—not just planning. Rather than waiting years to make that change, we chose to adopt a name that accurately reflects our long-term vision from the very beginning.

2. Why "The Wedding Business School"?

Because today's wedding industry is a thriving business ecosystem. Success requires expertise in planning, design, venues, hospitality, marketing, technology, operations, finance, entrepreneurship, and client experience. Our programs are built to prepare students for opportunities across this entire ecosystem, making The Wedding Business School a more accurate reflection of what we offer.

3. Has anything changed apart from the name?

Our commitment to excellence remains unchanged. Students will continue to experience our signature residential learning model, live wedding exposure, industry mentorship, and practical, business-focused education. The change is one of identity, not quality or purpose.

4. What is The Wedding Business School?

The Wedding Business School is a premium institute dedicated to training aspiring wedding professionals through a practical, industry-focused curriculum.

5. Who can join this course?
  • Fresh graduates
  • College students
  • Working professionals
  • Entrepreneurs
  • Homemakers restarting their careers
  • Event & hospitality professionals
  • Anyone passionate about the wedding industry

No prior wedding planning experience is required.

6. What is the minimum qualification required?

Candidates should have completed at least PUC/12th Standard or an equivalent qualification. Graduates and professionals from any educational background are welcome.

7. Do I need prior experience in wedding planning?

Not at all! Our curriculum is designed for beginners as well as professionals who wish to upskill or transition into the wedding industry.

8. What will I learn during the program?
  • Wedding planning & management
  • Luxury & destination weddings
  • Client consultation & management
  • Budgeting & financial planning
  • Vendor management
  • Wedding design & styling
  • Logistics & timelines
  • Contracts & documentation
  • Marketing, branding & business
  • AI tools for wedding professionals
  • And much more!
9. Is the training practical?

Absolutely! At The Wedding Business School, learning goes beyond the classroom. Students participate in:

  • Live wedding projects
  • Practical assignments
  • Venue visits
  • Vendor interactions
  • Wedding simulations
  • Industry workshops
  • Internship opportunities
10. Will I receive a certificate?

Yes. Students who successfully complete the program receive a Professional Certification from The Wedding Business School.

11. Is placement assistance provided?

Yes. The Wedding Business School provides placement assistance through its industry network. We actively support students with interviews, internships, portfolio building, and career guidance.

12. Can I start my own wedding planning business?

Yes. We guide you on starting your own business — from branding and pricing to client acquisition and vendor networking.

13. Is this course only for women?

No. The wedding industry welcomes talented professionals of all genders.

14. Is there an age limit?

Yes — 18 is the minimum. Beyond that, there's no upper limit. Students from different age groups have successfully built careers in the wedding industry.

15. Is accommodation available?

Yes. The Wedding Business School offers residential learning options, allowing students to immerse themselves in a real wedding environment while learning.

16. Are meals included?

Residential batches include meals during the training period. Specific details are shared upon admission.

17. Is a laptop mandatory?

Yes. Students are required to bring a laptop for assignments, presentations, budgeting exercises, planning software and project work.

18. What makes The Wedding Business School different from other institutes?

We focus on real wedding exposure, industry mentors, practical learning, luxury wedding expertise, small batch sizes, personalized mentorship, entrepreneurship training, placement assistance and a strong industry network.

19. Who are the faculty members?

Our faculty consists of experienced wedding planners, designers, venue professionals, hospitality experts, entrepreneurs and industry specialists with years of practical experience.

20. How long is the course?

The duration of the program is 30 days.

21. Are seats limited?

Yes. To ensure personalized mentoring and hands-on learning, each batch has limited seats.

22. How do I apply?

Send your resume to apply@theweddingbusiness.school to make an application. Our admissions team will review your application and get in touch with you.

23. Can international students apply?

Yes. Students from India and abroad are welcome to apply, subject to eligibility and visa requirements where applicable.

24. Will I get internship opportunities?

Yes. Eligible students receive opportunities to intern with wedding planners, venues and industry partners, subject to availability and performance.

25. Can I work as a freelancer after completing the course?

Absolutely. Many graduates begin as freelance wedding planners, coordinators, designers or consultants before establishing their own businesses.

26. What career opportunities are available after completing the program?

You can work as a wedding planner, coordinator, consultant, stylist, designer, venue manager, operations manager, vendor manager, bridal consultant, entrepreneur and more.

27. Why should I choose a career in the wedding industry?

The wedding industry offers exciting opportunities to combine creativity, management, hospitality and entrepreneurship. It provides diverse career paths with the potential to work on memorable celebrations, build a personal brand or establish your own business.

28. How can I contact The Wedding Business School?

You can reach our admissions team through the contact details provided on our website or Instagram page. We're happy to answer your questions and guide you through the admission process.

29. Do you offer scholarships or financial assistance?

At present, The Wedding Business School does not offer scholarships or financial assistance. Our focus is on delivering a premium, hands-on learning experience with exceptional industry exposure and personalized mentorship.

30. Can I visit the campus before enrolling?

Absolutely. We encourage prospective students and parents to visit our campus to experience the learning environment firsthand. Campus visits are available by prior appointment.

31. Is there an alumni network?

As a growing institution, we are in the process of building a strong Wedding Business School Alumni Network that will foster lifelong professional connections, collaboration, mentorship, and industry opportunities for our graduates.

32. Will I build a professional portfolio during the course?

Yes. Throughout the program, you will work on practical assignments and live projects that help you build a professional portfolio to showcase your skills and enhance your career prospects.

33. Do you teach destination wedding planning?

Yes. Destination wedding planning is an integral part of our curriculum, covering planning, logistics, hospitality, vendor coordination, budgeting, guest experiences, and execution.

34. What software and AI tools will I learn?

The Wedding Business School features a dedicated AI learning module designed specifically for the wedding industry. You'll learn how to leverage AI for planning, design, client presentations, budgeting, content creation, productivity, and business growth using industry-relevant digital tools.

35. Is English mandatory?

Not at all. Fluency in spoken English is not a requirement. A basic understanding of English sufficient to follow instructions and use digital applications is all that's needed. Passion, creativity, and a willingness to learn matter far more than language fluency.

36. What should I bring for the residential program?

Please bring:

  • Your laptop (mandatory)
  • Personal essentials and clothing
  • Any prescribed medications or personal care items

We'll take care of the rest so you can focus on learning and creating.

37. Are there EMI or payment options available?

At present, The Wedding Business School does not offer EMI or installment payment options. The fee is payable as per the admission guidelines shared with selected candidates.

38. What is the selection process?

Our admissions process is designed to ensure every student is committed to building a successful career in the wedding industry.

  1. Email your resume to apply@theweddingbusiness.school
  2. Our admissions team will review your application and get in touch.
  3. You will be invited for a counselling and evaluation interaction.
  4. Based on the interaction, shortlisted candidates will be selected.
  5. Selected applicants will receive an admission offer and can confirm their seat by completing the enrollment process.

This curated selection process helps us maintain small batch sizes and deliver personalized learning and mentorship.

39. Is it mandatory to stay on campus? Can I go home during the program?

Our program is designed as a residential learning experience to maximize collaboration, practical exposure, and team-based learning. Since many workshops, live wedding setups, and group assignments may extend into the evenings, staying on campus is strongly recommended.

However, the weekly schedule is shared in advance, allowing you to plan your personal commitments. Depending on the training schedule, you may choose to visit home during your free time, provided it does not affect your participation in the program.

40. Will there be a reduction in the fee if I choose not to stay on campus?

No. Accommodation is reserved for every enrolled student as part of the residential program. As the curriculum includes collaborative assignments and practical sessions that may continue into the evening, students are expected to stay on campus whenever required by the schedule.

Since the residential facilities are allocated for every enrollment, the course fee remains the same irrespective of whether you choose to utilize the accommodation.

41. Will I continue to receive support after completing the program? What happens after I graduate from here?

Yes. As a graduate of The Wedding Business School, you become part of our alumni community. You'll continue to benefit from alumni networking opportunities, industry updates, exclusive events, mentorship sessions, and ongoing guidance as you build and grow your career in the wedding industry.
